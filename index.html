Holiday scams: a Pittsford guide to staying safe

Simple steps to help you, your family, and neighbors avoid the most common holiday scams.

For Pittsford and Monroe County residents Based on guidance from the FTC, FBI, and law enforcement
Online deals and fake stores

Too good to be true holiday sales

Many scams start with a social media ad or email for a hard to find toy or gadget at a deep discount. The site looks real, reviews are copied from elsewhere, and the timer says the deal ends soon.

Slow down and check

  • Search the store name with the word scam or reviews in a separate tab.
  • Look for a real street address and working customer service phone number.
  • Pay with a credit card, never wire transfers, Zelle, or crypto for a new seller.
  • Be wary of sites that only take gift cards or ask you to pay a stranger directly.
Tip: If a popular item is half the price of every major retailer, assume it is fake until you can prove otherwise.
Gift cards and payment apps

"Pay right now with a gift card"

Scammers often pretend to be a family member, tech support, or a government agency. They create panic, then demand payment with gift cards or a payment app.

Remember these rules

  • No real business or government office asks you to pay taxes, bail, or fees with a gift card.
  • Never read gift card numbers over the phone or text them to anyone who contacted you first.
  • Stop and independently call the person or agency using a number you trust, not one from the text or email.
Local note: If someone in Pittsford is being pressured to pay this way, encourage them to hang up and call the Monroe County Sheriff non emergency number for guidance.
Package delivery and text scams

Text messages about missed deliveries

Fake delivery texts claim a package is waiting and link to a site that steals card numbers or personal information.

How to stay safe

  • Do not click the link in the text or email.
  • Go directly to the official UPS, FedEx, USPS, or retailer website and sign in there.
  • Check the tracking number from your original order confirmation instead of the text.
Quick checklist

Before you send money or information

1
Pause. Scammers rely on pressure and urgency. Take a breath and slow the conversation down.
2
Verify. Use a phone number or website you find yourself, not one sent in a text or message.
3
Talk it through. Check with a family member, trusted friend, or neighbor in Pittsford before you pay.

A five minute conversation can be the difference between a close call and a costly scam.

If you think you were scammed

Act quickly

  • Contact your bank or card company immediately and ask them to review charges.
  • Change passwords on any accounts that might be affected, especially email and banking.
  • Report the scam at ReportFraud.ftc.gov and to your local law enforcement agency.

In an emergency, call 911. For non emergency situations in Monroe County, use the non emergency number for the appropriate agency.

Share the information

Help neighbors stay safe

Consider sharing these tips with older relatives, new residents, and friends who are busy during the holidays. A quick reminder can prevent a costly mistake.
